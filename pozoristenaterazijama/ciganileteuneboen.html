GYPSIES FLYING INTO THE SKY
Director: Vladimir Lazić


A young man named Alexei Maksimovic son of a poor worker, an orphan who was los his father at young age, a boatman, a cook, a singer, a rogue and a self-taught man - he was twenty-four when he got into prison. It was 1892. At that time he had a pseudonym under which he was known by the world literature, Maxim Gorky in prison writes his first short story: While Cudré. Although the first, this short story has a thematic and an artistic side - it will serve many decades later as the basis for the film and musical Gypsies Flying into the sky. This novel show a classic form of "double exposure". While Cudré, the Gypsy, is talking while sitting coast, about his gypsy so called brother Lojka Zobar and his love for the beautiful Gypsy Rada. While Cudré is the topic for himself, the writer outlines his profile as a strong person, so that he himself could be the subject of a story. Gorki gives Makari the role of the narrator to speak about the history of a wondrous love. This history is an interesting narrative frame for a wide picture that the writer and his main character are painting in front of the reader. Written in Tiflis, in the apartment of the political runnaway Kaljužni, published in the newspaper "Kavkaz", she stands - chronologically and artistically - leading all collected books of Maxim Gorky. Gorki's Gypsies are romantic legends. Their author finds them in the desert, while wandering the endless spaces of homeland Russia.
