CABARET
Director: Chad Walker


Originally titled "Welcome to Berlin", "Cabaret" is based on the book "I am a Camera" by John Van Druten. The story "Cabaret" takes place in 1930 in Berlin. The play is about two pairs in love: the first are Sally Bowles, who works at a cabaret club "Kit Kat" and american writer Cliff Bradshaw, the other German, Ms. Schneider and her suitor, a Jew Schultz. The Broadway premiere of the musical was in November 1966 directed by Prince and choreographed by Ron Field, and the play was replayed in 1987. Based on a theatre performance in 1972. it was made into a film with Liza Minnelli in the lead role.
In the series of several musicals, first "Kiss Me Kate" , then "Korus line" and finally "Chicago", Terazije Theatre has succeeded in showing the key roles in the development of the musical. These three performances are actually a small part of the development of this kind of theatre. But after the premiere of "Chicago" shortly appeared certain dilemmas: whether to continue in the same direction, or choose the project in an entirely different estetic circle. The prevailing opinion is that it would be best to dig in the play. To commit and make phosie style of play more modern and make jazz ballet a more common ground and part of out theatre experience.
